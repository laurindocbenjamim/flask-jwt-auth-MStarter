Nigeria's journey into space began with the establishment of NASTA, the National Space Research and Development Agency in 1999.
Since then, Nigeria has launched five satellites, each playing a vital role in the country's development.
Take for example, Nigeria's SAT-1 launched in 2003 as part of the disaster monitoring constellation.
This satellite has been instrumental in tracking environmental changes, monitoring agricultural activities, and aiding disaster relief efforts.
These satellites helped monitor crop conditions during Nigeria's severe 2012 flooding, saving thousands of acres from disaster by providing farmers with timely data.
In 2011, Nigeria launched Nigeria's SAT-2 and Nigeria's SAT-X.
They both provide high-resolution images that support urban planning, resource management, and military surveillance.
Security was bolstered when these satellites were used for surveillance against Boko Haram insurgent groups, aid in military strategy and intervention in the region.
In transportation, satellite imagery has guided the expansion of critical highways, improving urban rural connectivity and supporting economic growth.
